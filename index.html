Mark Twain

A gifted racounter, distinctive humorist and irascible moralist.

Here's some interesting facts about Mark Twain

  • Born in 1835
    Mark Twain was born in Florida Missouri two months prematurely and was in relatively poor health for the first 7 years of his life. He wasn´t expected to live long. Mark Twains real name was Samuel Langhorne Clemens. Mark Twain as a pen name appears for the first time while working as a journalist at the Virginia City Newspaper 1863. Mark Twains was the sixth child out of seven. Only three of them survived early childhood.
  • Childhood
    Mark Twain grow up in Hannibal Missouri, a Mississippi River port town. The novels The adventures of Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn was influenced by his childhood.
  • Violence and death
    Life was rough for a young boy in 19th century. Death was always present in Mark Twains life. 1844 He discovered a corpse of a Californian emigrant stabbed in a quarrel in his fathers office, a self-educated lawyer, and was placed there for the inquest. 1845 He watched a man die in the street after he had been shot by a local merchant. This incident provided the basis for the Boggs shooting in Hucklebery Finn. 1847 He witnessed the drowning of one of his friends. A few days later when fishing he discovered a drowned an mutilated body of a fugitive slave.
  • The riverboat pilot
    As he grew up he tried many occupations. 1859 after failing as an miner he become a Mississippi Riverboat pilot. He also arranged a job for his younger brother Henry on a riverboat. Disaster struck when a boiler exploded and Henry dies at an age of 19 due to his injuries. Twain was guilt-stricken and held himself responsible for the rest of his life. The Carrier as a riverboat pilot ended when the Civil War broke out 1861.
  • The Novels and fame
    Between 1874 and 1891, he wrote his most classical novels. Twain become one of the world's most famous writers and earned a lot of money.
    • 1876 The adventures of Tom Sawyer
    • 1881 The Prince and the Pauper
    • 1883 Life on the Mississippi
    • 1884 Adventures of Huckleberry Finn
    • 1889 A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur´s court
  • His Poor investments
    Mark Twain had a genuine intressent in modern science. He become close friends with Nikola Tesla. Twain made large investments in new inventions and technologies which didn´t go well. He lost most of his wealth earned from writtings. It´s estimated Mark Twain lost about $9.000.000 in todays money value. He filed for bankruptcy in 1894. Later in life by giving lectures and writting for newspapers, also with the help from his friend Henry Rogers, a wealthy businessman in the oil industry, he managed to recover financially.
  • The depressed Mark Twain
    In his later years, Twain went through a period of deep depression when his daughter Susy died of meningitis in 1896 followed by the death of his wife's 1904. The year 1909 even deepened his gloom when his daughter Jean and his closest friend Henry Rogers dies the same year. Mark Twain passed away in 1910 in Redding Connecticut.
  • No direct descendant a live today
    Mark Twain married Olivia Langdon 1870 and the couple had four children including a son who died as a toddler and two daughters who passed away in their 20s. Olivia Clemens died 1904 at age 54 while 1910 Mark Twain passed away at age 74 the one surviving child, Clara after she died at age 88 1962 had one child, Nina Gabrilowitsch. Nina was childless so there are no descendants since Nina passed away 1966.
